Approach

Approach

Behaviour is the most honest data source available.

Not what people say, not what they intend — what they actually do, and the gap between the two.

People make their problems visible through their actions without realising it. The doctor who builds a workaround. The audience that ignores the homepage and goes straight to episode pages. The leadership team that keeps requesting the same report in five different formats because the underlying question has never been properly answered.
Principle 01

Start with the question, not the tool.

Before any implementation I run a structured discovery: what is the data not doing for you, what decisions are you trying to make, what do you think is stopping you. Most analysts start with the data they have. I start with the question the data should answer.

Principle 02

Build for adoption, not delivery.

A Loom training library, an A/B result that converts leadership, a pipeline that retires eight hours of manual work. The job isn't done when the implementation is live. It's done when the people around it can use it independently.

Principle 03

Work across the boundary.

Instrumentation is a technical discipline. User research is a qualitative one. Most people are strong on one side. Sense-Making Methodology training plus eleven Amplitude certifications is the combination that lets a measurement layer surface the human question underneath the number.
