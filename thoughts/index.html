Working archive

The question is the unit.

Each note starts as an unresolved measurement question. Some become cases. Some stay as method problems. That is the point.

Posted 4 Apr 2026Methodology / Sense-Making

Where does qualitative methodology replace quantitative instrumentation, not just supplement it?

The standard order is "instrument first, qualitative for the why". Cases like the shadow-IT discovery suggest the order sometimes inverts: observation first, instrumentation last. What is the rule for which way around it should go?

Working theory: the order inverts when the workflow is unauthorised, because instrumentation only sees what the system was designed to surface and never the workarounds users build on top. Quantitative discovery would only show the gap as silence.

Posted 18 Apr 2026AI-assisted workflows / Alert design

What is the right gate-structure for AI-assisted analytics workflows when human judgement is the bottleneck, not the model?

The V1 alert-triage system caught a critical issue humans had missed and still kept the human as the actor. V2 needs the same logic in a different shape. Where do the gates go when the AI is good enough that suppression becomes the harder design problem?

Working theory: the interesting design moves up the stack: taxonomy, schema, what counts as "the same error". The model is fine. The thing that needs design attention is the data layer the model is reading from.

Posted 1 May 2026Measurement design / Session continuity

What replaces sessions when users stop and start their visits across hours and days instead of minutes?

The session was useful when a visit looked like a bounded sitting: arrive, browse, convert or leave. Increasingly, the journey looks more like a thread picked up across tabs, devices, messages, notifications, saved carts, and delayed decisions. What unit of measurement survives when the user's actual visit no longer fits inside the analytics platform's clock?

Working theory: the replacement is not a longer session timeout. It is a task-state model: intent, interruption, resumption, and completion stitched across events. The unit is less "visit" than "open loop."

Posted 6 May 2026Schema design / Behavioural signals

How do you instrument for deliberate omission: a step the user actively skipped?

An expected event that does not fire reads as silence by default. The skip itself disappears into the gap. What schema-level move makes "the user chose not to do this" a first-class signal rather than an absence?

Suspect: the answer lives in the surrounding context: dwell time on the option, hover, back-out pattern, sequence of competing actions. The omission becomes visible as a positive signal once you instrument the choices made around it.

Posted 15 May 2026Practitioner observation / Discovery

What is the analytics function's own shadow IT?

If shadow IT in product teams is a signal that the designed system does not support a real workflow, where does the same pattern live inside the analytics function: the spreadsheets, the manual pulls, the workarounds practitioners build because the official measurement layer does not cover the question?

Suspect the answer is uncomfortably close to "everywhere." Open question: what would it mean to instrument the analytics function for its own gaps the way we instrument products for theirs?
