Evidence paths

The model first, then everything that makes it trustworthy.

Marketing measurement runs on the same sequence: resolve the identity, build the model, change how decisions get made, hold one shared set of definitions, keep the pipeline auditable, and bring in qualitative methods when the event data cannot explain a change. The cases below follow that order.

Path 01 · Marketing modelling Identity resolution · regression + allocation · budget shift

The identity layer that made the budget argument defensible.

Stanley Steemer's six channels sat in disconnected systems with no way to compare cost-efficiency. Automated multi-attribute hash blocking and union-find graph clustering resolved every customer into one identity. Census household data supplied a household-penetration measure, and a regression base plus a stakeholder-agreed weighted-allocation layer for passive channels compared every channel on the same economics. The sharpest finding was negative: mail personalisation had no measurable effect on response — changing the offer did.

Vinted fit · The team's mandate — maintain and improve media mix models, develop ROI and payback frameworks, advise Marketing leadership — is this case's shape: a production MMM whose output changed real budget decisions, with the problem-definition work done before the modelling.

Path 02 · Experimentation culture A/B testing · leadership adoption · 280 locations

Introducing A/B testing as a leadership practice.

Five years building marketing analytics from scratch at a 280-location franchise: the Azure SQL warehouse foundation, the single-customer and householding model, Universal Analytics API ingestion in Python, the GA4 beta bridge through BigQuery — and A/B testing introduced as leadership decision support. By year two, leadership was requesting evidence before decisions.

Vinted fit · Steering budget with models is a culture problem before it is a modelling problem. This is the record of making that shift stick with leadership that had run on instinct for decades.

Path 03 · Canonical definitions CDP migration · customer model · zero downtime

Replatformed the CDP. The campaigns did not notice.

An Oracle Responsys to Bloomreach CDP migration at a consumer electronics retailer where the customer data model, event schemas, segmentation, and automation logic were redesigned instead of copied forward — consolidating audience truth across count logic, deduplication, consent, email, and SMS, with zero campaign downtime and measured year-one engagement lift.

Vinted fit · When systems disagree on who the customer is, drive alignment toward one canonical definition rather than accommodating several — and hold it through a live migration.

Path 04 · Data engineering BigQuery · pipeline rebuild · cost discipline

Three raw scans to one. Fifteen CTEs to zero. ~50% cost reduction.

A second-pass audit of a GA4-to-BigQuery reporting layer: a roughly $112/run cascading CTE chain redesigned around a single conditional-aggregation pass, a 1.2 TB intermediate table retired, channel-grouping logic unified into a single definition, and scheduled-query costs cut by roughly half — with a four-phase migration that required no reporting freeze.

Vinted fit · The role's day-to-day stack is Python, SQL, dbt, and a BI tool. This is the engineering discipline underneath trustworthy model inputs: canonical logic defined once, pipelines that are cheap enough to run and simple enough to audit.

Path 05 · Mixed methods GA4 · UserZoom · Sense-Making methodology

Telemetry locates. Method diagnoses.

At the same 280-location franchise, GA4 telemetry showed exactly where users dropped off but not why. A Sense-Making-informed UserZoom protocol diagnosed the gap types the funnel could not see — including an A/B variant that was winning despite a vocabulary problem, and a decision journey that began upstream of the homepage.

Vinted fit · The advisory half of the Decision Scientist role is explaining results to Marketing leadership, including the limits of what a result can claim. This case is that discipline applied in practice.
