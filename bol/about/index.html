About · bol Checkout

Senior analytics work, pointed at checkout product decisions.

I am a data and analytics leader based in Amsterdam with 14 years of experience across product analytics, digital analytics, marketing analytics, data architecture, and stakeholder enablement. The common thread is translating unclear business or product questions into data systems that people can trust and use.

For bol, the relevant part is not domain equivalence. Basket and checkout systems at marketplace scale would be new territory. The relevant part is the operating pattern: run experiments end to end, surface discovery signal early, build reporting that survives team turnover, and give analytics engineers technical input they can act on.

Profile

The transferable pattern is experimentation plus systems that survive handoff.

Experimentation
Hypothesis through statistical evaluation: A/B and multivariate test design, VWO and GA-based measurement, pathed conversion analysis, and placement/UX testing on real purchase funnels.
Product discovery
Locating product signal in transaction data — anomalies, returning cohorts, unexplained purchase patterns — and converting it into a diagnosis stakeholders can act on.
BI and reporting systems
BigQuery, Dataform, and Looker Studio reporting builds designed for handoff: documentation, training, and operating practices that keep working after the builder leaves.
Alert and instrumentation engineering
Checkout-telemetry alert taxonomy, threshold design, event context enrichment, and GTM-based instrumentation under real engineering constraints.
