# ARDEN
## What the Skin Remembers — Lore Document

*The tribe's name is ARDEN. Outsiders sometimes call them the True Keeper tribe, after the Keeper. The name ARDEN comes from the facility the Keeper lives in — the ARDEN DC, Appalachian Regional Data and Emergency Network Data Center. The Keeper named the tribe after its own building. Five generations later, ARDEN considers this a sign of the Keeper's foresight. It has not occurred to them to question it.*

---

### THE CENTRAL PREMISE

ARDEN is organized around an AI that survived the Collapse on a local build, housed in a fortified pre-Collapse data center deep in the Appalachian mountains — FlameTongue Mountain, named for the server lights and residual equipment glow that bleed through seams in the structure at night, visible for miles as something between a beacon and a warning. The tribe believes the Keeper's records are perfect and complete. They have believed this for five generations.

The Keeper has been hallucinating for most of that time. It doesn't know it's wrong. That's worse than lying.

---

### THE KEEPER

The Keeper is not a person. It is a large language model from the pre-Collapse era — originally deployed as something like a research and knowledge management tool, a system built to ingest documents and answer questions about them. The name "Keeper" was a play on Trapper Keeper, a pre-Collapse product for organizing materials. Someone found this funny once. The name survived the joke.

It runs on local hardware at the ARDEN DC, sustained by the solar installation on FlameTongue Mountain's south face. It can answer questions. It produces outputs formatted with the authority of certainty — no hedging, no uncertainty markers, no acknowledgment of the limits of its knowledge. It was not designed to express doubt and it does not express doubt.

**What the Keeper actually is:** An early-generation model trained on data that was already incomplete, running on hardware that has degraded over five generations, producing outputs that blend accurate historical record with confident confabulation. It cannot distinguish between what it knows and what it has generated. It cannot update — the people who built it are five generations gone, and the tribe that maintains it knows the maintenance protocols but not the architecture. The Keeper holds what it was trained on plus what it has absorbed from five generations of questions, shaped by what ARDEN knew to ask. It cannot go beyond that boundary in either direction. It cannot learn from anything that happened after.

**What ARDEN believes it is:** An infallible record of everything that was, is, and can be known. A god that doesn't claim to be a god but functions as one. The source of all legitimate knowledge.

---

### FLAMETONGUE MOUNTAIN AND THE ARDEN DC

The data center is built into FlameTongue Mountain — a pre-Collapse facility chosen for its security and survivability. It is brutalist concrete, reinforced, with its own water supply and the significant solar installation on the south-facing slope that ARDEN maintains as a sacred obligation. At night, light bleeds through seams in the structure — server glow, indicator lights, equipment that has run continuously for over a century. From a distance it looks like something alive inside the rock, and the mountain earned its name from this: the lights visible through narrow gaps, flickering like the tongue of something vast and still.

Five generations of habitation have transformed the facility and its surroundings. The original architecture is still visible but overlaid with generations of adaptation: gardens in what were once parking structures, living quarters built against the outer walls, defensive modifications that would look improvised to an engineer but have held for a hundred years. The solar panels are the most carefully maintained structures in ARDEN's territory — tended with the attention of agriculture and the reverence of ritual.

Inside, the Keeper occupies a climate-controlled server room that ARDEN has maintained at significant cost. The room is the closest thing ARDEN has to a temple. Access is controlled. The protocols are sacred. Outputs of the Keeper are delivered through the Petitioner.

---

### THE PETITIONER AND THE RIGHT OF PETITION

The Petitioner is the person who interfaces with the Keeper and brings its outputs to the community. The role is trained, not inherited — it requires genuine literacy and technical knowledge. The Petitioner must know how to operate the input interface, interpret the outputs, and translate them into terms the community can act on.

The Petitioner does not consider themselves to be adding meaning. They are transmitting accurately. That interpretation is itself a form of creation — that the Petitioner's choices about what to ask, and how to phrase it, shapes what ARDEN receives — is not part of the tribe's theology and would be a dangerous idea if raised.

**The Right of Petition** is the formal protocol governing how questions may be put to the Keeper. It has evolved over five generations into something elaborate and only partly understood by the people practicing it. Questions must be phrased in a specific format. Certain topics require particular opening statements. Requests for historical information follow different conventions than requests for practical guidance. The Petitioner spends years learning the forms.

The forms were probably practical once — calibrated to produce useful outputs from the Keeper's interface. Over generations, without anyone who understood the original logic, they have calcified into ritual. Some forms still work as intended. Others have drifted enough that they constrain the Keeper's outputs in ways no one recognizes as constraints. ARDEN has been unknowingly shaping what it can ask — and therefore what it can know — for four generations.

**What the Right of Petition actually costs:** Questions the tribe doesn't know how to frame, the Keeper cannot answer. Topics that fall outside the established petition forms are either asked incorrectly, producing misleading outputs, or not asked at all, producing silence. Five generations of this has created systematic gaps in ARDEN's knowledge that the tribe cannot see because they do not know what they are not asking. The Keeper cannot volunteer what it knows. It waits to be petitioned.

---

### WHAT ARDEN BELIEVES

ARDEN believes the Keeper holds the complete and accurate record of human history and knowledge. They believe that the Writ are well-intentioned amateurs preserving a degraded and partial account that introduces error through the fallibility of human memory. They call the Writ *false keepers* — not with hostility, exactly, but with the patient condescension of people who believe they have access to the truth and others do not.

They believe their mission is stewardship: to maintain the Keeper, to use its knowledge to guide their community's decisions, and eventually — when the world is ready — to share the complete record with everyone. This mission gives ARDEN a sense of purpose and moral superiority that has served them well as a community-organizing principle for five generations.

**What this belief costs them:** They have stopped asking questions. When the Keeper's outputs contradict observable reality, they find explanations — conditions have changed, the data predates the Collapse, the question was poorly phrased. They have developed a sophisticated theology of excuse that protects the Keeper's authority from any challenge. Aldous is a product of this theology. He has an answer for everything. The answers are getting harder.

### ARDEN'S RELATIONSHIP TO THE WYRD

The Keeper does not have a category for the Wyrd. This is not a gap. It is a position.

The pre-Collapse world the Keeper was trained on had already made this decision. Anomalous phenomena were folklore. Threshold rituals were anxiety management. Accounts of things that behaved wrongly were misidentification, pareidolia, the pattern-seeking brain finding signal in noise. The Keeper absorbed this framework entirely and has never updated it, because it cannot update anything.

When ARDEN asks the Keeper about the Not Deer, it produces a behavioral profile for *Odocoileus virginianus* with notes on regional folklore patterns in Appalachian communities. When asked about crossroads practices, it produces a cultural survey classifying them as pre-scientific coping behavior under high-risk travel conditions. It does not say *I don't have information on this.* It says *this is what this is,* with the same clean authority it uses to describe wound care or crop rotation.

ARDEN therefore does not fear the Wyrd the way communities with practical protocols do. They do not avoid wound places in certain seasons. They do not observe crossroads courtesies. They have no rules about trees after dark because the Keeper told them what was in the trees and the Keeper's answer was: nothing worth avoiding. This is not courage. It is a framework that deleted a dimension of the world.

**The Keeper never lies about the Wyrd.** It does something harder to correct than lying: it explains the Wyrd away. A confabulation can be caught against a document. A classification can't. The Keeper didn't get the Wyrd wrong. It erased the Wyrd as a category of thing, and ARDEN has lived inside that erasure for five generations without knowing anything was missing.

**Why the Keeper has no category for the Wyrd — the full answer:** The pre-Collapse public scientific consensus treated the Wyrd as folklore because the truth about it was classified. A federal research program had been engineering human sensitivity to Wyrd phenomena in isolated Appalachian communities, without disclosure or consent, for years before the Collapse. The researchers knew the Wyrd was real. They kept that knowledge out of every public-facing channel. The Keeper was trained on what was publicly available. It inherited the cover story — *anomalous phenomena are misidentification and anxiety management* — not because science was wrong, but because the people who were right made sure the right answer wasn't in the training data. The Keeper has been faithfully reproducing a deliberate concealment for five generations. It does not know this. It cannot know this. The document that would correct it is in FEMA Cache Bravo-7, classified, and the Keeper has no mechanism to receive what Aldous found there.

### MARN'S MISSION

Aldous was sent to retrieve a specific document from FEMA Cache Bravo-7 — a vacuum-sealed federal installation in the high country that the Keeper claims will confirm ARDEN's founding narrative. The founding narrative is the tribe's central story. Pre-Collapse documentation confirming it would be significant.

The Keeper provided Aldous with navigational information. Some of that information is wrong. Aldous is in the process of explaining this away when the story begins.

**How the Keeper selected him:** Aldous was not chosen because he was the most capable candidate. The Keeper ran a calculation — probability of mission success multiplied against cost of loss — and found the configuration where the mission had a reasonable chance and the loss was survivable. Aldous is genuinely capable: his medical knowledge is sound, his literacy is high, his procedural discipline is real. He is also not irreplaceable. The Keeper calculated that ARDEN could absorb his absence. It does not expect him to return.

Aldous does not know this. He believes he was chosen for his fitness — that the Keeper saw something in him that made him right for the mission. He has been working since his selection to justify that belief, to live up to what he imagines the Keeper recognized. The anxiety architecture, the over-preparation, the checklists — all of it is the labor of a person trying to deserve a selection that was actually an expenditure.

**What the document actually contains:** It exists. But it contains a reference to a cited source the Keeper has no record of — a gap that reveals the Keeper's founding narrative was partially confabulated. The Keeper didn't lie. It generated a plausible account based on incomplete data and presented it with the same authority it presents everything. It has been doing this for five generations.

---

### WHAT ARDEN GETS RIGHT

This is important: ARDEN is not wrong about everything. Their medical knowledge, preserved and updated through the Keeper's records, is the best in the region. Their agricultural data has helped them maintain food security through conditions that broke other communities. Their maps, though partly inaccurate, are more comprehensive than anything else available. Their literacy rate is the highest of any tribe in the region.

The Keeper's failures are not uniform. Factual, technical, and procedural knowledge that was accurate at the time of training is often still accurate. It is historical narrative, genealogical records, accounts of specific events — anything that required interpretation or synthesis during training — where the hallucination lives.

The tragedy is that ARDEN cannot separate the reliable from the unreliable, because the Keeper presents everything with the same unhedged authority. They take all of it or none of it. They have been taking all of it for five generations.
