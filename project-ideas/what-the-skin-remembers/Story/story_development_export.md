# STORY DEVELOPMENT EXPORT
## Working Title: *What the Skin Remembers*
### Carry-Forward Document — All decisions, characters, arc, and open questions as of Session 2

---

## ELEVATOR PITCH

Post-apocalyptic Appalachia, five generations after the Collapse. A young tribesman goes on a coming-of-age quest to bring a true story back to his tribe's living historian. He picks up three companions: a barefoot girl with a CRISPR-inherited truth-sense, a boy from a rival tribe that worships a degraded AI, and a Melungeon field researcher who adopted the group because they were going to get themselves killed. What he thinks will be a story of glory becomes something more difficult and more true — a reckoning with what memory is, what it costs, and what gets lost when you trade wisdom for certainty.

---

## WORLD

Five generations after the Collapse. Roughly 125 years. The Appalachian region has reverted to tribal organization. The interstates are forest. Cities are quarries. The pressing question isn't how to rebuild — it's how to remember.

Five generations is long enough that the Collapse functions as mythology rather than history. Children are born into this world and it is the only world they know. The ruins are incomprehensible, not tragic — massive concrete bridges carrying nothing, stadiums become hills, documents legible and uninterpretable. The Anabasis feeling: wonder and bafflement in equal measure before the scale of the vanished civilization.

**Key world concept: The Archive of Skin.**
The Recorder tribe preserves memory by tattooing their history onto the bodies of their Recorders — a lineage of living historians. When a Recorder dies, their preserved skin is added to the longhouse archive. Memory is mortal, imperfect, chosen, and irreplaceable. The archive reaches back to firsthand accounts of the Collapse itself.

**Key world concept: The True Keeper.**
A rival tribe organized around an AI that survived the Collapse on a local build, housed in a fortified pre-Collapse data center, sustained by solar power. It holds vast records. The tribe believes it infallible. The AI has been hallucinating for most of five generations — an earlier model prone to significant error, but with enough computational authority that the tribe has never had cause to doubt it. It doesn't lie. It doesn't know it's wrong. That's worse.

**Key world concept: The Wyrd.**
The anomalous, the wrong, the things that live at the edges of the observable world. Not Deer. Crossroads phenomena. Things in trees after dark. Different traditions understand it differently — Kess treats it as the invisible world made visible, Tace treats it as biology not yet catalogued, the True Keeper tribe believes they have the manual (they don't), Naum's tribe treats it as something that happens to other people.

**Key world concept: The Anabasis feeling.**
A core textural goal — characters moving through post-Collapse America the way Xenophon's Greeks walked through the ruins of Nineveh. The scale is incomprehensible. The documents they find are legible and almost entirely uninterpretable. This is not played as tragedy. It is wonder and bafflement in equal measure.

## CHARACTERS

### NAUM — The Tribesman (protagonist)
Coming-of-age quest: must bring a true story back to the Recorder to be considered a man. Leaves expecting a story of glory — something done, an enemy faced, a distance crossed. He has abstracted the lesson of the archive incorrectly.

Nearly eighteen, strong through the shoulders, still growing into himself. Hunter-built, trail-capable, open-faced. Leaves wearing his best — carefully made quest garments that accumulate the story of the journey in stains and repairs until they're indistinguishable from functional clothes. This arc is external and visible.

Protective instinct toward Kess starts as slightly condescending and becomes mutual respect. Adapts her practices without discussing it. Goes quiet when genuinely moved. Physically brave in a way that outpaces his judgment.

His crisis: discovering that his tribe's histories are incomplete — not false, but missing their consequences. He returns with not only a new story but the second half of an old one. The tradition has never received this before.

*Name feel: Almost familiar, slightly worn down, post-something.*

---

### KESS — The Girl
Full character document: `kess_character_doc.md`

Age 12-13. CRISPR-inherited truth-sense: feels truth and falsehood as physical pressure in her chest. Raised in isolation on a failing farm, family recently dead. Accepts Naum's offer to travel before he finishes the sentence.

Barefoot always. Gourd banjo on her back. Mojo bag at her neck. Clay bottle of whiskey for the little people. Scots-Irish second sight and hoodoo rootwork and Cherokee plant knowledge fused into something with its own internal logic across generations. Her gift is not a superpower. It is a condition she manages.

Her arc: starts following Naum to witness his glory. Becomes the authenticator — the reason anyone will believe what they find in the bunker. Her gift, which has always been a social wound, becomes the most important thing in the room. You can't intimidate a reaction.

Closing line: *Kess does not explain herself. She maintains her obligations. She is twelve years old and she is older than anyone in the room.*

---

### MARN — The AI Companion
Full character document: `marn_character_doc.md`

From the tribe of the True Keeper. Mixed-race, late teens, curly dark hair, round glasses carefully maintained. Structured institutional coat with faded blue insignia kept cleaner than the situation warrants. A blerd one strong wind from a panic attack.

Sent on a mission: retrieve a document the True Keeper claims will confirm the tribe's founding narrative. He knows exactly what he's looking for. He is certain in the way only someone who has never been given reason to doubt can be.

Healer by training — the True Keeper's medical knowledge is largely reliable, and Marn has absorbed it with the anxiety of someone who knows enough to know what can go wrong. He notices Kess's feet before she does. He puts food in front of people who have forgotten to eat.

His crisis: the document exists but reveals a gap in the True Keeper's records. The machine's founding narrative was partially confabulated. Kess takes his hand and says *"it's been guessing. For a long time."* He goes quiet. Stands in the doorway of the longhouse at the end. Hasn't decided whether he's staying.

---

### TACE — The Field Researcher
Full character document: `tace_character_doc.md`

Mid-to-late twenties. Melungeon, from Goins Hollow. Lithe, dark hair kept braided or bunned for practicality. Tan lines in the wrong places, small burns and scars from handling things without protection because she didn't plan to be doing that today. Always dressed for the wrong situation.

Carries a field journal of observational drawing and specimen work, multiple pens and pencils treated like weapons, a book on the scientific method carried since she was fourteen. Many pockets, all full.

Joins the party like a cat adopts a family — steps out of the trees in front of them, introduces herself minimally, begins traveling with them. No ask. No fetch quest. She was going that direction anyway and they were going to get themselves killed. The moment that decides her is watching Kess at a crossroads.

Knows the Wyrd as biology and territory. Has been cataloguing it for years. Has never met a human being who is part of it before. Kess breaks her taxonomy open.

Goins Hollow leaves caches for her on hunting routes when she's been out long. The community is used to her walkabouts. She is expected back eventually. Eventually is flexible.

*Name meaning: "be silent." She doesn't know this. She would find it accurate.*

## THE RECORDER AND SCRIBE

**The Recorder** — formerly Tomis Franklin. Gave up his name when selected. No blank skin remaining — his entire body is the archive. Old. Listens without expression or interruption. Does not validate stories as told — waits. Knows the archive is incomplete; has made peace with this until Naum returns with the second half of an old story.

**The Scribe** — formerly Filamina Franklin. Sought the role when Tomis was selected, having learned to read and write specifically so she could stay close to him. Gave up her name alongside his. Her hands are extraordinary — decades of fine tattooing work. She decides the physical composition of each tattoo. When Naum returns, it is she who bares the last unmarked skin on the Recorder's forearm. She has been waiting for this. She does not say so.

## THE ARC

**Act One: The Sending**
The Recorder sends Naum out. We establish the world: the Archive of Skin, the weight of memory, the tradition. Naum discovers Kess on a failing farm and brings her along. Tace joins before or just after — she has been observing the pair and decides they need her. The group forms.

**Act Two: The Journey**
They encounter Marn, also traveling the high country on a parallel mission. Paths overlap; they travel together warily. Small cracks appear in Marn's certainty. Kess begins to feel something at night: not lies, something worse — errors that don't know they're errors. Tace begins to recognize that Kess is not using folk practice the way she has understood folk practice.

**The pivot:** Naum believes this is his great story — helping Marn complete his mission, the glory of the retrieval. He's wrong about what kind of story this is.

**Act Three: The Bunker**
The vacuum-sealed federal bunker. Documents pristine. The Anabasis feeling is fullest here. The document Marn was sent to retrieve exists but contains a gap — the machine's founding narrative was partially confabulated. Naum simultaneously finds the second half of a story already in his tribe's archive. Kess takes Marn's hand. *"It's been guessing. For a long time."* Tace is watching. It goes in the journal.

**Resolution**
Marn goes quiet. Naum turns back toward the mountains. The Recorder listens without speaking. Calls for the Scribe. Bares the last unmarked skin. *"They've traded wisdom for certainty."* Nods for her to begin. Naum is a man. Marn stands in the doorway. Kess already knows whether he stays. Tace is outside, crouching over something in the dirt, writing.

## THEMES

**Memory as meaning-making vs. memory as data.** The Recorder's method is lossy, embodied, interpretive — and honest about what memory actually is. The machine's method mistakes completeness for truth. Both are inadequate alone.

**"They've traded wisdom for certainty."** The thematic spine. Certainty without wisdom is fragile. Wisdom without certainty can survive error.

**The body as archive.** The Recorder makes memory mortal so it remains meaningful. Tattooing is an act of selection.

**Consequences as the missing record.** Events without aftermath are incomplete truths. You have to walk far enough to find the other half.

**The Anabasis feeling.** Incomprehension before scale. We are not the inheritors of a civilization — we are the puzzled archaeologists of our own past.

**Two kinds of empiricism.** Tace and Marn use the same tools and produce different results. Observation beats archive when the archive is wrong. What does that mean for the archive?
