Bio

Bio

Analytics architecture, with the question kept close to the build.

Fourteen years building the measurement layer that lets organisations stop guessing: data models, tracking instrumentation, KPI frameworks, dashboards, and the adoption work that makes any of it useful.

Most recently as Analytics Architect at Telus Digital, I have worked across enterprise clients in consumer electronics, B2B SaaS, e-commerce, and travel. The work usually sits between product questions and data infrastructure: implementation plans, event schemas, SQL pipelines, dashboard layers, and the handoff that lets teams use the system after the build.

Before Telus, I spent five years at Stanley Steemer building marketing analytics from scratch across 280 franchise locations: an Azure SQL Server foundation, identity-resolution work, customer and household modelling, A/B testing practice, and a modernised measurement layer for marketing and digital teams.

I have worked under HIPAA on protected data and alongside enterprise consent and privacy regimes including OneTrust and TrustArc. Compliance and consent design sit inside the instrumentation work, not as an after-the-fact bolt-on. That does not make me a medical-device specialist; it does mean regulated data constraints are familiar operating conditions.

My usability research practice is built on Sense-Making Methodology paired with the standard practitioner toolkit: task-based testing, think-aloud protocols, heuristic evaluation, SUS, task-success metrics, observational research, and neutral questioning. The qualitative side of the work is not an appendix to the quantitative side; it is how the right telemetry questions get formed.

I am drawn to contexts where the measurement layer has to survive contact with real behaviour: workarounds, omissions, partial adoption, ambiguous signals, and teams trying to make decisions with imperfect data. That is the useful transfer into UX telemetry: not pretending the dashboard is the work, but making the system trustworthy enough for product teams to use.

On the people side: I managed two designers at Micro Center during a CRM migration and have mentored junior analysts informally across several engagements. That is real but scoped people-management experience; senior IC work remains the strongest fit today.

Based in Amsterdam. EU work-authorised. Currently full-time at Telus Digital; standard notice applies.
