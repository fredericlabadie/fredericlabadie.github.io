Bio

Bio

Analyst, architect, and the person you call when the data won't behave.

Fourteen years building the analytics layer that lets organisations stop guessing — data models, tracking instrumentation, KPI frameworks, dashboards, and the experimentation culture that makes any of it useful.

Most recently as Analytics Architect at Telus Digital, working across enterprise clients in consumer electronics, B2B SaaS, e-commerce, and travel — typically automating 12+ hours per week of manual reporting per client through SQL pipeline development.

Before Telus, five years at Stanley Steemer building marketing analytics from scratch across 280 franchise locations: greenfield, built on an SSMS / Azure SQL Server foundation, with the ageing BI stack retired. Introduced the single-customer concept and householding data model that gave the business its first reliable identity resolution across the multi-location estate. Introduced A/B testing as a leadership practice; by year two, leadership was actively requesting data-informed decisions.

I've worked under HIPAA on protected data, alongside enterprise consent and privacy regimes including OneTrust and TrustArc. Compliance and consent design sit inside the instrumentation work, not as an after-the-fact bolt-on — relevant for a regulated medical-device context.

My usability research practice is built on Sense-Making Methodology paired with the standard practitioner toolkit — task-based testing, think-aloud protocols, heuristic evaluation, SUS and task-success metrics. The qualitative side of the work, not an afterthought to the quantitative side. Applied across three employers: focus group facilitation at Planned Parenthood (informing the Melody Health brand launch, where the subscriber base grew from 20k to 50k), UX question design at Designory/TBWA, and user testing at Stanley Steemer.

I'm drawn to high-stakes contexts because stress and constraint strip away performance — what remains is signal. I work with teams that want their data to change their decisions, not just confirm them. In clinical adjacencies, that orientation is the default.

On the people side: managed two designers at Micro Center on the CRM migration, and mentored junior analysts informally across several other engagements. Real but scoped people-management experience; team leadership at scale is the next thing to grow into, not something already done.

Based in Amsterdam. EU work authorisation. Currently full-time; open to the right next role with standard notice.
