Mixed-Methods UX Research.

Quantitative telemetry and SMM-informed qualitative research at a national home services franchise — a method for diagnosing gap type, not just gap location.

Frederic Labadie · Stanley Steemer (anonymised) · 2021–2022

Why this is in the Philips selection
The single most-relevant case in the library for the UX side of the brief. The four SMM neutral-questioning design rules (anchored to specific moments, asking about the gap, not presupposing the bridge type, verb-framed not noun-framed) are a protocol-level transfer to clinical UX research. The argument — that analytics and qualitative research are sequential rather than competing methods — is the same shape the Adoption & Insights division needs when a clinical workflow surfaces a usage anomaly: telemetry locates where, SMM diagnoses what kind of gap, design responds appropriately.
→
What happened
At a 280+ location home services franchise, quantitative GA4 telemetry was paired with a UserZoom protocol that was ~90% SMM-informed neutral questioning — producing a documented sequential protocol (telemetry locates · SMM diagnoses · design responds) and three findings of three different gap types that the funnel could not see: a problematic gap in category consideration (cleaning categorised as treat, not maintenance), a confusion gap at the promotion step (a winning A/B variant won despite a vocabulary problem), and a pre-arrival sense-making finding (the funnel begins upstream of the homepage). The case is the methodology piece behind every SMM reference elsewhere on the site, and the basis for the talk "Finding the Hypothesis When You Only Know the Question". Below: the long-form anatomy in six sections.
01
Problem
Strong telemetry, blind to gap type

Problem

The data showed where users dropped off. It couldn't show why.

A national home services franchise with 280+ locations had strong digital traffic but underperforming conversion in specific service categories. The telemetry was precise — we knew exactly where users dropped off. What it could not tell us was the nature of the gap users were experiencing at those moments.

The question driving the research programme: are users failing to convert because of a barrier gap (something is blocking them), a decision gap (they cannot choose between options), or a problematic gap (something feels wrong but they cannot name it)?

Without diagnosing the gap type, any conversion optimisation would be designing bridges for assumed gaps rather than real ones.

02
Methodology
Two streams, designed to work together

Methodology

Quantitative telemetry paired with SMM-informed qualitative research.

Two parallel research streams, designed to work together — one to locate where gaps appear, the other to diagnose what kind of gap is actually present.

Stream 1 · Quantitative telemetry
GA4 event data, funnel analysis, A/B testing, session behaviour. Identifies where gaps appear and which interventions move metrics. Cannot identify gap type or the sense users are trying to make.
Stream 2 · Sense-Making sessions (UserZoom)
Moderated sessions with a question protocol that was roughly 90% SMM-informed questions and 10% instructions. Every question designed to surface information was built on SMM neutral questioning principles.
SMM neutral-questioning protocol · design rules
Every question in the UserZoom protocol was constructed against four constraints:
  • Anchored to a specific moment rather than general patterns — "the last time you needed your carpets cleaned" not "how do you feel about carpet cleaning".
  • Asked about the gap (what stopped you, what were you uncertain about) rather than about the topic itself.
  • Did not presuppose the bridge type — questions never implied that "information" or "a special offer" was what users needed.
  • Framed around verbs (what were you trying to do?) not nouns (what information did you need?).

Users were given minimal scaffolding — a blank page and a brief scenario description — to observe genuine sense-making behaviour rather than primed responses.

This is not a common approach in commercial UX research. Most UserZoom protocols ask satisfaction questions or task-completion questions. SMM-informed questions ask about the situation, the discontinuity, and what would have helped users move forward.

03
Findings
Three findings · three gap diagnoses

Findings

What the SMM-informed sessions surfaced that the funnel could not.

Finding 01 · Problematic gap Category consideration · pre-funnel

Professional cleaning is categorised as a treat, not maintenance.

When users described their cleaning decision-making without priming, professional cleaning services did not appear in their primary consideration set for routine cleaning. Users defaulted to DIY or retail products. Professional cleaning appeared only when the task felt beyond DIY scope, or as a reward.

SMM diagnosis. This is not a barrier gap (something blocking them from booking) — it is a problematic gap. Users do not have a clear sense of when professional cleaning is the appropriate response. The category has not been defined in their sense-making structure as a maintenance behaviour.

Strategic implication. Conversion optimisation at the booking step addresses the wrong gap. The gap is earlier — at category consideration, not at purchase decision.

Finding 02 · Confusion gap Promotional step · vocabulary research

The vocabulary gap that A/B testing could not surface.

A multi-round A/B and UX test examined promotional display approaches: no promotion visible, a pop-up promotion, and a pulsing promo code.

The quantitative result. Pulsing promo codes increased user attention and engagement significantly over the no-promotion baseline.

The UserZoom finding. Users were confused by the distinction between "Promo Code" and "Online Specials" — two terms the organisation used interchangeably but that users read as categorically different things. This confusion was not visible in the A/B test data. The winning variant was winning despite the vocabulary problem, not because of clean messaging.

SMM diagnosis. Users were experiencing a confusion gap at the promotion step — not a decision gap between options, but a sense-making failure around what the promotion category meant. Fixing the variant without fixing the vocabulary would have optimised an impaired bridge.

Sense-Making / Dervin Confusion gap A/B test reading Vocabulary research
Finding 03 · Pre-arrival sense-making Search behaviour · SEO & content strategy

Search behaviour precedes site arrival.

User research on cleaning decision triggers revealed that the sense-making process for finding a cleaning service begins well before landing on the site. The gap — "I need my home cleaned" — is crossed partially before users reach the digital funnel.

Strategic implication. This shaped how we thought about SEO and content strategy: the site needed to meet users at an earlier point in their sense-making journey, not just at the booking decision. The funnel begins upstream of the homepage.

04
Protocol
Telemetry locates · SMM diagnoses · design responds

Combined protocol

The value was in the sequence.

Neither stream produced the insight on its own. The protocol is the relationship between them.

01
Telemetry identifies
Quantitative telemetry surfaces that a gap exists at a specific funnel step. The funnel data tells us where to look — it cannot tell us what we will find when we look.
02
SMM diagnoses
SMM-informed qualitative research diagnoses the nature of the gap at that step — barrier, decision, problematic, confusion. The diagnosis names what kind of sense-making is breaking down.
03
Design responds
The diagnosis determines what kind of bridge to build. A barrier gap needs removal; a confusion gap needs clarification; a problematic gap needs reframing earlier in the journey.

Without the telemetry, the research would have no anchor — we would not know where to look. Without the SMM research, the telemetry would produce optimised bridges for the wrong gaps.

This is the argument I continue to develop in the talk "Finding the Hypothesis When You Only Know the Question — Without Moving the Goal Posts": quantitative analytics and qualitative research are not competing methods. They are sequential ones.
