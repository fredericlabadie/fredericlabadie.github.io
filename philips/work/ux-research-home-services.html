Case study · Path 01 · Question formulation & discovery

Telemetry locates. Method diagnoses.

Quantitative telemetry and Sense-Making-informed qualitative research at a national home services franchise: a method for diagnosing gap type, not just gap location.

Frederic Labadie · Stanley Steemer (anonymised) · 2021–2022

Role-fit signal
The UX side of the telemetry cycle. The Philips role asks for teams to define the right questions before those questions become tracking plans. This case shows the method behind that step: telemetry identifies where behaviour changes; Sense-Making-informed research diagnoses what kind of gap the user is experiencing; design responds to the diagnosis instead of the metric alone.
→
What happened
At a 280+ location home services franchise, quantitative GA4 telemetry was paired with a UserZoom protocol built around Sense-Making-informed neutral questioning. The result was a documented sequence: telemetry locates · method diagnoses · design responds. The research surfaced three different gap types the funnel could not explain: a problematic gap in category consideration, a confusion gap at the promotion step, and pre-arrival sense-making that began upstream of the homepage.
01
Problem
Strong telemetry, blind to gap type

Problem

The data showed where users dropped off. It could not show what kind of gap they were in.

A national home services franchise with 280+ locations had strong digital traffic but underperforming conversion in specific service categories. The telemetry was precise: we knew where users dropped off. What it could not tell us was the nature of the gap users were experiencing at those moments.

The research question was not simply “why are users failing to convert?” It was more specific: are users facing a barrier gap, a decision gap, a confusion gap, or a problematic gap they cannot yet name?

Without diagnosing the gap type, conversion optimisation becomes guesswork: building bridges for assumed gaps rather than real ones.

02
Methodology
Two streams, designed to work together

Methodology

Quantitative telemetry paired with Sense-Making-informed research.

One stream located the gap. The other diagnosed the kind of gap. The value was in the sequence.

Stream 1 · Quantitative telemetry
GA4 event data, funnel analysis, A/B testing, and session behaviour. This identified where gaps appeared and which interventions moved metrics. It could not identify the gap type or the sense users were trying to make.
Stream 2 · Sense-Making sessions
Moderated UserZoom sessions with a protocol built around SMM-informed neutral questioning. The questions were designed to surface the situation, the discontinuity, and what would help the user move forward.
SMM neutral-questioning protocol · design rules
Every question in the UserZoom protocol was constructed against four constraints:
  • Anchor to a specific moment rather than a general pattern: the last time you needed your carpets cleaned, not how you feel about carpet cleaning.
  • Ask about the gap: what stopped you, what were you uncertain about, what were you trying to move through?
  • Do not presuppose the bridge type: avoid implying that “information,” “a special offer,” or “better navigation” is already the answer.
  • Frame around verbs: what were you trying to do, not what information did you need?

Users were given minimal scaffolding: a blank page and a short scenario description. The point was to observe sense-making behaviour, not prime a response.

Most commercial UX protocols ask satisfaction or task-completion questions. SMM-informed questions ask about the situation, the discontinuity, and the bridge the user needed but did not have.

03
Findings
Three findings · three gap diagnoses

Findings

What the SMM-informed sessions surfaced that the funnel could not.

Finding 01 · Problematic gap Category consideration · pre-funnel

Professional cleaning was categorised as a treat, not maintenance.

When users described cleaning decisions without priming, professional cleaning services did not appear in the primary consideration set for routine cleaning. Users defaulted to DIY or retail products. Professional cleaning appeared when the task felt beyond DIY scope, or as a reward.

SMM diagnosis. This was not a barrier gap. It was a problematic gap: users did not yet have a clear sense of when professional cleaning was the appropriate response.

Strategic implication. Conversion optimisation at the booking step addressed the wrong gap. The gap lived earlier: category consideration, not purchase decision.

Sense-Making / Dervin Problematic gap Category consideration Pre-funnel research
Finding 02 · Confusion gap Promotional step · vocabulary research

The vocabulary gap the A/B test could not see.

A multi-round A/B and UX test examined promotional display approaches: no visible promotion, a pop-up promotion, and a pulsing promo code.

The quantitative result. Pulsing promo codes increased user attention and engagement significantly over the no-promotion baseline.

The UserZoom finding. Users were confused by the distinction between “Promo Code” and “Online Specials”: two terms the organisation used interchangeably but that users read as categorically different things. The winning variant was winning despite the vocabulary problem, not because the message was clear.

SMM diagnosis. Users were experiencing a confusion gap, not a decision gap. Fixing the variant without fixing the vocabulary would have optimised an impaired bridge.

Sense-Making / Dervin Confusion gap A/B test reading Vocabulary research
Finding 03 · Pre-arrival sense-making Search behaviour · SEO & content strategy

The funnel began upstream of the homepage.

Research on cleaning decision triggers showed that the sense-making process began before users reached the site. The gap — “I need my home cleaned” — was crossed partially through search and content exposure before the digital funnel began.

Strategic implication. SEO and content strategy had to meet users earlier in the sense-making journey, not only at the booking decision.

Sense-Making / Dervin Pre-arrival research SEO & content strategy Decision-trigger research
04
Protocol
Telemetry locates · SMM diagnoses · design responds

Combined protocol

Neither stream produced the finding alone.

Neither stream produced the insight on its own. The protocol was the relationship between them.

01
Telemetry identifies
Quantitative telemetry surfaces that a gap exists at a specific funnel step. The funnel data tells us where to look; it cannot tell us what we will find when we look.
02
SMM diagnoses
SMM-informed qualitative research diagnoses the nature of the gap at that step: barrier, decision, problematic, confusion. The diagnosis names what kind of sense-making is breaking down.
03
Design responds
The diagnosis determines what kind of bridge to build. A barrier gap needs removal; a confusion gap needs clarification; a problematic gap needs reframing earlier in the journey.

Without the telemetry, the research would have no anchor. Without the SMM research, the telemetry would produce optimised bridges for the wrong gaps.

This is the argument I continue to develop in the talk “Finding the Hypothesis When You Only Know the Question — Without Moving the Goal Posts”: quantitative analytics and qualitative research are not competing methods. They are sequential ones.
