Path 02 · primary evidence

Instrumentation & Cross-Platform Telemetry

The layer the rest of the telemetry cycle depends on: event schemas, implementation requirements, cross-platform parity, SDK debugging, error taxonomies, and monitors that can be trusted when product teams need to act.

Maps to the brief: data needs, event definitions, tracking plans, implementation coordination, and dashboard-ready telemetry.

Compact · Error taxonomy Major quick-service restaurant · Amplitude monitors

Clean taxonomy, trustworthy alerts, enriched context.

Three layers of remediation on a noisy alert channel: threshold redesign, a hierarchical error-category ID scheme (SYS-INF-001, SYS-APP-VAL-010), and cart_id context enrichment. The principle is simple: error events need the same diagnostic context as their success counterparts.

Role fit · Event taxonomy, alert engineering, and telemetry context design — the foundations for dashboards and AI-assisted triage that teams can trust.

Compact · SDK debugging Major quick-service restaurant · Segment · analytics-swift 1.9.1

A silent iOS data-loss bug, traced to the initialisation sequence.

Case · GTM architecture B2B/B2C hardware authentication brand · e-commerce instrumentation · 2016

All analytics in the tag manager. Not one line in the site code.

Security requirements banned analytics code from the site codebase entirely — all tags, variables, and triggers had to live in GTM. When the purchase funnel became a Single Page Application mid-build, the constraint stayed fixed: DOM-based variables, wildcard matching, history-state pageviews, and enhanced ecommerce delivered from the container alone.

Role fit · Advanced GTM architecture under constraints: the same design discipline behind translating data requirements into production telemetry without coupling the measurement layer to the site build cycle.

Off Adobe, onto Google. Three phases and a cleared backlog.

Adobe Analytics had made the client’s tracking backlog unmanageable — every change required developer lift. Path 04 · primary evidence

AI Workflow Design

The model is not the interesting part. The interesting part is the workflow: where AI assists, where human judgement stays accountable, and what prevents automation from becoming unexamined evidence.

Maps to the brief: AI-enabled telemetry use cases that strengthen product understanding and decision-making.

Case · AI workflow design Major quick-service restaurant brand · analytics architect

Designing AI workflows that do not outsource the thinking.

A production alert-triage system with explicit gates around where AI assists and where human judgement is preserved. V1 ran 13–26 April 2026; 230 raw alerts became 39 posts, an 83% reduction, and one critical feature failure surfaced that the on-call team had missed.

Role fit · AI surfaces and ranks; it does not diagnose or decide. That gate-design discipline is the transferable part for high-stakes telemetry contexts.

Case · AI platform telemetry Fuel iX · TELUS Digital enterprise GenAI platform

Product analytics for an enterprise GenAI platform.

The platform had backend billing metering but no front-end behavioural signal. A GA4 event design and BigQuery export pipeline added that layer — with a query primer equipping the engineering team to answer product questions independently. Pipeline went live and into engineering use within a week.

Role fit · Designing the measurement layer for an AI product — event schema, BigQuery export, engineering enablement — so the team building the product can answer usage and behaviour questions without routing every data question through analytics.

Path 01

Question Formulation & Discovery

The work before the event spec: defining the decision, locating the gap, and pairing behavioural data with the user context needed to interpret it.

Maps to the brief: workshops, UX telemetry questions, usability affinity, and the method behind measurement design.

Case · Mixed-methods methodology National home services franchise · 2021–2022

Telemetry locates. Method diagnoses.

GA4 and A/B telemetry located where users fell out. A UserZoom protocol built around Sense-Making-informed neutral questioning diagnosed what kind of gap it was. The resulting protocol treated analytics and qualitative research as sequential methods, not competing ones.

Role fit · The usability and human-factors bridge: telemetry can locate friction, but the method determines what the friction means.

Adoption & Cultural Transition

A measurement layer has not been adopted because it exists. It has been adopted when the team changes how it works, keeps using it, and can explain the system without the original builder in the room.

Maps to the brief: data storytelling, data literacy, and the cultural transition toward evidence-based product management.

The reporting layer survived the people who built it.

BigQuery, Dataform, and Looker Studio reporting infrastructure replaced recurring manual report pulls, paired with a three-session structured handoff so the client team could maintain and extend the system independently.

Role fit · Adoption measured in use, not delivery: 38 analyst hours recovered per month and zero data-infrastructure ramp-up after turnover.

Case · Placement A/B testing B2B/B2C hardware authentication brand · 2016

The quiz worked. The question was where to put it.

A product selection quiz was quietly driving conversion — but only for users who found it. Phase 1 GA instrumentation confirmed the signal. Phase 2 VWO A/B testing across four store-page placement variants confirmed that prominence was the variable: the prominent entry-point variant roughly doubled the pathed conversion rate.

Role fit · A/B test design and instrumentation that translates a UX hypothesis into a measurable outcome. The method — confirm the signal, then test the surface — transfers to placement decisions in any telemetry-equipped product.
