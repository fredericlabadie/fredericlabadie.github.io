A workaround broke. The transaction data confessed.

Philips relevance · Observational research + neutral questioning surfacing a gap the designed system never knew it had. Directly transferable to clinical-context discovery, where the gap between designed workflow and lived practice is exactly what UX research needs to surface.

Topic 04 · primary mapping to the Philips brief

AI Workflow Design

The interesting work in AI-assisted analytics isn't the model — it's the gates. Where AI assists, where human judgement is preserved, and why those lines are where they are.

For the Philips brief: directly mapped to the AI-assisted analytics agenda inside Adoption & Insights.

Case · AI workflow design Major quick-service restaurant brand · analytics architect

Designing AI workflows that don't outsource the thinking.

Philips relevance · The most directly transferable case in the library. AI surfaces and ranks — it does not diagnose or decide. The same gate-design discipline applies to any clinical-context AI workflow where outsourced thinking is the failure mode.

A production alert-triage system with explicit gates around where AI assists and where human judgement is preserved. V1 ran 13–26 April 2026; compressed 230 raw alerts to 39 posts (83% reduction), surfaced one critical feature failure the on-call team had missed in their human-only review.

Topic 05

Adoption & Cultural Transition

The final measure of an engagement isn't what got built — it's whether the team changed how they work. A measurement layer that produces reports nobody reads has not been adopted.

For the Philips brief: the principle behind enabling internal Philips teams to own their measurement.

Case · Enablement & handoff State-regulated retail platform · analytics architect · 2025–2026

The system survived the people who built it.

Philips relevance · Same shape as enabling internal Philips teams to own their measurement — documentation built for the team that inherits the system, not for the team that built it. Zero ramp-up after turnover is the right benchmark for any handoff into a permanent organisation.

Greenfield UX telemetry across web, app, and CRM — then a three-session structured handoff designed so the client team could maintain and extend it independently. 38 analyst hours recovered per month, zero ramp-up time when the team turned over.

Topic 07

Data Engineering & Architecture

Pipeline architecture, cost-thinking, schema design, and the patient work of stepping back to look at the whole system — not just writing faster queries.

For the Philips brief: the systems-thinking that scales to large telemetry estates.

Case · Cost engineering State-regulated retail platform · analytics architect · April 2026

Three raw scans → one. Fifteen CTEs → zero. ~50% cost reduction.

Philips relevance · Architectural redesign of a GA4 → BigQuery pipeline through cost-thinking and DAG restructuring. The same systems-thinking and economic discipline scale to any large telemetry estate — Philips included.

An audit and architectural redesign of a GA4 → BigQuery pipeline that had grown into a 1.2 TB intermediate table and a $112/run cascading CTE chain. Single conditional aggregation replaces ~18 TB of repeated scans. ~50% cost reduction across pipeline and dashboard queries.
