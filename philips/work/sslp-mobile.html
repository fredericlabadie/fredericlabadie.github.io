Case study · Path 02 · Instrumentation & cross-platform telemetry

From "what should we measure?" to 30+ shipped tickets.

Greenfield mobile UX telemetry across iOS and Android: spec to production, with cross-platform parity, QA, and consent built in before launch.

/ State-regulated retail platform · analytics architect · 2025–2026

Role-fit signal
End-to-end telemetry implementation, from question to production. The work covered schema design, event definitions, SDK implementation, QA, cross-platform parity, dashboard-ready data, and consent management through TrustArc and OneTrust. That is the same delivery shape the Philips role describes: translate UX and product questions into implementable tracking plans, then coordinate the work until the telemetry is real.
→
What happened
A state-regulated retail platform shipped a new mobile app with a full UX telemetry layer underneath it: 30+ tickets authored, owned, and deployed across iOS and Android, cross-platform schema parity audited and closed, GA4 e-commerce taxonomy live, and consent management integrated before launch. The dashboards worked because this layer did.
The deliverable was not a dashboard. It was the measurement layer the dashboards would eventually depend on: taxonomy, schema, SDK implementation, QA, and production deployment across both platforms, with consent governance integrated before launch instead of retrofitted after.
Situation
A state-regulated retail platform was building a brand-new mobile app from scratch. Zero prior instrumentation: no taxonomy, no schema, no SDK integration, no production deployment story for telemetry. Downstream marketing, product, and compliance work all depended on getting the foundation right.
Task
Stand up the mobile telemetry layer end to end across iOS and Android, from specification to production, with cross-platform parity audited explicitly and consent management built in from the start. The work had to be usable by engineering, product, analytics, and governance stakeholders.
Action
Designed the GA4 e-commerce taxonomy, screen-view coverage, interaction telemetry, retention cohort support, QA process, and governance path. The implementation work moved through Jira, real-device testing, GA4 DebugView validation, regression checklists, privacy review, and data-governance sign-off before launch.
Result
The mobile app launched with a telemetry foundation the analytics team could trust on day one. Downstream marketing and product work could build against it without months of retroactive reconciliation, and the consent-aware design avoided the common debt of trying to make unsafely collected data usable later.
Event schema design
Full GA4 e-commerce taxonomy (purchase, add_to_cart, view_item, begin_checkout, view_cart, refund) with required items array, value, currency, transaction_id. Screen-view coverage across core, auth, permission, and system-state screens.
Interaction telemetry
Bottom-nav events, search-query submission with results count, PDP product metadata on every event, barcode-scanner interactions with full payload, account-management flows, and retention cohorts in GA4 Explore.
QA & governance
GA4 DebugView for real-time validation, real-device testing across iOS and Android, regression QA checklists per sprint, privacy-compliance review before any PII-adjacent property was specced, and data-governance sign-off from client stakeholders.
