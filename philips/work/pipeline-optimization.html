Case study · Path 07 · Data engineering & architecture

Three raw scans to one. Fifteen CTEs to zero. ~50% cost reduction.

A second-pass audit of a GA4-to-BigQuery reporting layer built after the initial BI enablement work. Claude and BigQuery MCP supported inspection of repeated scans, a 1.2 TB intermediate table, and an approximately $112/run cascading CTE chain; the architectural decisions and migration plan stayed human-owned.

Role-fit signal
Scalable telemetry needs architectural cost thinking. The Philips role depends on telemetry estates that remain reliable as usage grows. What happened
A GA4-to-BigQuery analytics pipeline had grown into a roughly $112/run CTE chain processing approximately 18 TB per execution. I redesigned the pipeline around a single conditional-aggregation pass, retired the 1.2 TB GA4_Event_Params intermediate table, and cut scheduled-query costs by roughly half.
The optimisation was architectural, not query-level. Pipeline debt accumulates one reasonable decision at a time. The work was not to tune a single query; it was to step back and redesign the shape of the system.
Situation
The first pass had already replaced a recurring manual reporting cycle with a BigQuery, Dataform, and Looker Studio reporting layer. The next question was cost and maintainability. The pipeline scanned events_* three separate times, unnested every event type into a generic key-value table, then ran 15+ separate CTEs over that 1.2 TB table and full-outer-joined them back together.
Task
Audit the whole data flow, identify where the costs were really coming from, and propose a migration plan the team could execute without freezing the reporting layer. Cost reduction was the visible target; maintainability and consistency were the real ones.
Method
Used Claude with BigQuery MCP to inspect schema shape, query structure, scan patterns, and candidate simplifications while keeping the architecture, validation plan, and migration sequence human-owned. The key move was replacing repeated independent scans with a single event-level computation that produced a flat, analysis-ready row.
Result
The result was cheaper, faster, easier to maintain, and more consistent: channel-grouping logic defined once instead of repeated in slightly different forms, and two intermediate tables retired without information loss. Cost reduction was the visible win; architecture was the durable one.
01
Single raw scan
Read the needed GA4 event rows once instead of repeating the same scan for each parameter extraction.
02
Conditional aggregation
Used MAX(IF(ep.key = 'x', ep.value.string_value, NULL)) to pivot 25+ event parameters simultaneously.
03
Canonical logic
Moved channel grouping into one definition instead of maintaining three slightly different CASE statements.
04
Partition + migrate
Added partitioning to large reporting tables and delivered a four-phase rollout with a 2–4 week validation window.
