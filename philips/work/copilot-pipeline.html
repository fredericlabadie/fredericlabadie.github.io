Case study · Path 04 · AI Workflow Design

Product analytics for an enterprise GenAI platform.

Adding the front-end measurement layer to Fuel iX, TELUS Digital’s enterprise GenAI platform. The product had backend metering for billing, but not behavioural telemetry for product and engineering questions.

/ Fuel iX · TELUS Digital enterprise GenAI platform · agency-client engagement

Role-fit signal
Instrumentation design for an AI product. The Philips brief asks for AI-enabled analytical opportunities and telemetry that supports product understanding. This case is that discipline applied: event design, BigQuery export, and engineering enablement so the team building the product could answer usage and behaviour questions without routing every data question through analytics.
→
What happened
An enterprise GenAI platform gained a front-end behavioural analytics pipeline that went live and into active engineering use within a week. The product had backend billing metering; now it also had behavioural telemetry for product and engineering questions.
Backend metering can confirm usage at billing level. It cannot explain which agents are being used, by whom, how often, or what changed after a release. Behavioural telemetry for AI products starts with the same event design questions as any other product.
Situation
Fuel iX had scaled from internal tool to commercial GenAI platform. Backend metering existed for billing. No front-end behavioural signal layer existed to answer which agents and copilots were used, by whom, how often, or what product behaviour looked like across organisations.
Task
Design GA4 event collection covering platform-wide and organisation-specific behavioural events, deliver BigQuery export, and equip the engineering team to query the data independently rather than waiting on an analyst for every answer.
Action
GA4 event design and BigQuery export pipeline delivered. A query primer — event-parameter unnesting, schema model, and example KPI queries including copilot count and conversations per user — made the data legible to the engineers building the platform. Internal BigQuery SME role continued after delivery.
Result
Pipeline live and in active engineering use within a week. GA4 querying primer created for the engineering team. Measurement advisory extended to a related platform tool on its own measurement layer.
