Adyen · Senior Product Data Analyst · Checkout Product

Product data should do more than report what happened.

It should clarify what to build, where operations break, which metrics teams can trust, and what systems need to scale. That is the overlap between the Adyen role and my work: turning complex product and commercial questions into durable data models, automated reporting layers, and decision surfaces people actually use.

The evidence below stays close to the existing portfolio: BigQuery pipeline optimisation, reporting enablement that survived team turnover, greenfield mobile instrumentation, SDK debugging, AI-assisted alert triage, and the longer arc of establishing analytics standards from scratch.

Fit map

The spine is product data ownership.

Adyen is not looking for standard reporting. The role emphasises senior ownership, product strategy, scalable data solutions, central sources of truth, and influence across engineering and business teams. The strongest evidence is therefore systems-oriented, not just analysis-oriented.

01 · Own complex initiatives
Pipeline redesign, greenfield instrumentation, and reporting enablement all required defining the approach, coordinating implementation, and carrying the work through to production or adoption.
02 · Build scalable systems
The clearest proof is architectural: repeated scans collapsed, manual reporting automated, shared definitions created, and downstream teams given data surfaces they could build on.
03 · Influence decisions
The through-line is not dashboard production. It is evidence that changes how teams decide: leadership requesting data before decisions, analysts inheriting maintained systems, and product teams trusting the telemetry layer.
