Current Meditations

What I've been thinking about lately.

A practitioner's working-thinking archive — drafts, working theories, open questions on methodology, AI-assisted workflows, schema design, and the gaps the official measurement layer doesn't see. Reflective, not finished. If any of these resonate, get in touch.

Posted 4 Apr 2026 Methodology · Sense-Making

Where does qualitative methodology replace quantitative instrumentation, not just supplement it?

The standard order is "instrument first, qualitative for the why". Cases like the shadow-IT discovery suggest the order sometimes inverts — observation first, instrumentation last. What's the rule for which way around it should go?

Working theory: the order inverts when the workflow is unauthorised, because instrumentation only sees what the system was designed to surface and never the workarounds users build on top. Quantitative discovery would only show the gap as silence.

Posted 18 Apr 2026 AI-assisted workflows · Alert design

What's the right gate-structure for AI-assisted analytics workflows when human judgement is the bottleneck, not the model?

The V1 alert-triage system caught a critical issue humans had missed and still kept the human as the actor. V2 needs the same logic in a different shape. Where do the gates go when the AI is good enough that suppression becomes the harder design problem?

Working theory: the interesting design moves up the stack — taxonomy, schema, what counts as "the same error" — not down into the model. The model is fine. The thing that needs design attention is the data layer the model is reading from.

Posted 1 May 2026 A/B testing · Pre-mortems

Why do most A/B tests die before they ship?

Working hypothesis: the question wasn't worth the test, and the cost of figuring that out post-spec is what kills the rollout. What's the upstream instrumentation that would have surfaced "not worth testing" before the spec was written?

Most test kills look like budget kills or scope kills, but those are downstream symptoms. The actual kill is a discovery gap. The instrumentation question is whether you can measure your way to "this isn't worth testing" without running the test.

Posted 6 May 2026 Schema design · Behavioural signals

How do you instrument for deliberate omission — a step the user actively skipped?

An expected event that doesn't fire reads as silence by default. The skip itself disappears into the gap. What's the schema-level move that makes "the user chose not to do this" a first-class signal rather than an absence?

Suspect: the answer lives in the surrounding context — dwell time on the option, hover, back-out pattern, sequence of competing actions — not the missing event itself. The omission becomes visible as a positive signal once you instrument the choices made around it.

Posted 15 May 2026 Practitioner observation · Discovery

What's the analytics function's own shadow IT?

If shadow IT in product teams is a signal that the designed system doesn't support a real workflow, where does the same pattern live inside the analytics function — the spreadsheets, the manual pulls, the workarounds practitioners build because the official measurement layer doesn't cover the question?

Suspect the answer is uncomfortably close to "everywhere." Open question: what would it mean to instrument the analytics function for its own gaps the way we instrument products for theirs?
