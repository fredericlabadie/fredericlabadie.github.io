Case study · Lens 06 · Marketing Analytics & Lifecycle

Replatformed the CRM. The campaigns didn't notice.

Full Oracle Responsys → Bloomreach CDP migration — customer data model, event schemas, segmentation, and triggered-campaign architecture all redesigned. Zero campaign downtime through cutover.

/ Consumer electronics retailer · lifecycle marketing · 2023–2025

→
What happened
A consumer-electronics retailer's CRM moved from Oracle Responsys to Bloomreach with the customer data model redesigned for the new platform's primitives, not lifted from the old schema — and with zero campaign downtime through cutover. Year-one results post-migration: +10.8% CTR YoY and +5.8% open rate YoY. The lift validated the architectural decision, not just the platform change. Same engagement included managing a team of two designers on the campaign-creative side.
Marketing-platform migrations fail two ways: by breaking campaign continuity (data gaps that don't close cleanly post-migration), or by carrying the previous platform's data model forward unchanged (winning the migration, losing the architectural opportunity). The work here did neither. The migration was the moment to redesign the customer data model, not the moment to deploy a fresh copy of the old one.
Situation
A consumer-electronics retailer running lifecycle marketing on Oracle Responsys, with a customer data model shaped by the platform's constraints — static list-based segmentation, identity stitching glued on, consent state managed sideways. The decision had been taken to move to Bloomreach. The risk: a fresh copy of the old schema in a new house.
Task
Run the migration without breaking campaign continuity and use the moment to redesign the customer data model around Bloomreach's CDP primitives. Two outcomes had to hold simultaneously: no campaign downtime through cutover, and no porting of the old architecture forward. The migration was the architectural opportunity; treating it as a platform swap would have squandered it.
Action
Customer data model rebuilt from scratch for Bloomreach — customer identity stitched cleanly across email, SMS, and on-site behavioural events, with consent state carried through the model rather than bolted on after. Lifecycle event taxonomy authored from product, transactional, and engagement signals. Segmentation logic rewritten to be queryable rather than enumerated — cohorts defined by behaviour rather than by static list membership. Triggered-campaign automation built on the new lifecycle event tracking, with GDPR-compliant consent maintained through cutover. Detail in the three-card breakdown below. Same engagement included managing a team of two designers on the campaign-creative side: real, scoped people-management.
Result
Year-one engagement lift was the evidence that the architectural call held: the platform swap on its own would not have moved the numbers — the data model designed for Bloomreach's primitives did. The CRM ended on infrastructure designed for what it now does, not what its predecessor used to do; the team that owned it could speak Bloomreach instead of translating from Responsys every release.
