Case study · Topic 06 · Marketing Analytics & Lifecycle

Replatformed the CRM. The campaigns didn't notice.

Full Oracle Responsys → Bloomreach CDP migration — customer data model, event schemas, segmentation, and triggered-campaign architecture all redesigned. Zero campaign downtime through cutover.

/ Consumer electronics retailer · lifecycle marketing · 2023–2025

Marketing-platform migrations fail two ways: by breaking campaign continuity (data gaps that don't close cleanly post-migration), or by carrying the previous platform's data model forward unchanged (winning the migration, losing the architectural opportunity). The work here did neither. The migration was the moment to redesign the customer data model, not the moment to deploy a fresh copy of the old one.
Customer data model
Redesigned from scratch for Bloomreach's CDP primitives — not lifted from the Responsys schema. Customer identity stitched cleanly across email, SMS, and on-site behavioural events, with consent state carried through the model rather than bolted on after.
Event schemas & segmentation
Lifecycle event taxonomy authored from product, transactional, and engagement signals. Segmentation logic rewritten to be queryable rather than enumerated — cohorts defined by behaviour rather than by static list membership.
Triggered campaign architecture
Triggered-campaign automation built on the new lifecycle event tracking — the behavioural signal the segmentation logic queries against. GDPR-compliant consent management maintained through cutover via the same configuration regime.

Outcome
A CRM platform on a customer data model designed for the new platform's strengths, not ported from the previous one. CTR and open-rate lift in year one were the evidence that the architectural decisions held — not that the platform itself moved the numbers. Same engagement included managing a team of two designers on the campaign-creative side of the migration: real, scoped people-management experience.
