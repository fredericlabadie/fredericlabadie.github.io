Connecting email behaviour to in-app sessions.

The instrumentation backbone behind every "did this campaign actually drive product interaction, or just a click?" question.

// 01 / thesisclicks are not the full journey

Core argument

A campaign click is not a product outcome.

The measurement layer needed to connect CRM behaviour to what users actually did after they landed in the product.

What happened

The campaign and product analytics layers got a shared identity spine.

A state-regulated retail platform's email campaigns and product analytics now share a unified identity layer: Braze ID and Account GUID pushed into the GA data layer, with Braze ID also carried in email URL parameters.

This made it possible to answer whether a campaign drove product interaction after the click, not only whether the email generated a click. The spec became the basis for recurring monthly executive readouts on user behaviour and product-engagement trends.

Context

The trail fragmented after the click.

The client used Braze for email and GA4 for product behaviour. Campaign clicks ended at the click. Once the user landed in the app or on the web, the trail of who they were and what they did fragmented across systems.

Task

Design a consent-aware bridge between CRM and product analytics.

The work was to specify the identity fields, URL parameter behaviour, data-layer placement, and tag configuration needed to connect email behaviour to app and web sessions without bypassing consent governance.

// 02 / identity architectureBraze / GA4 / data layer / consent

Architecture

The bridge lived in the data layer.

Identity stitching is not a dashboard trick. It has to be designed upstream, in the events and parameters that make the dashboard possible.

1Braze ID in URLs

Specified Braze ID carriage in email URL parameters so the campaign identity could persist beyond the email click.

2Account GUID in GA

Specified Account GUID and Braze ID in the GA data layer to connect product sessions to CRM identity context.

3GTM audit

Audited tag configuration so the new identity fields could move through the measurement layer cleanly.

4Consent guardrail

Kept tag configuration consent-aware under TrustArc so the identity bridge respected governance requirements.

Action

The spec made executive user-journey questions answerable.

I authored the specification to push Braze ID and Account GUID into the GA data layer and to carry Braze ID in email URL parameters. The same engagement covered GTM audit, TrustArc consent-aware tag configuration, and the recurring monthly executive readout cycle the new identity layer powered.

// 03 / resultunified journey / executive readouts / consent-aware

Result

The question changed from did they click to what did they do next.

The identity layer gave campaign analytics a product-behaviour endpoint, not just email-platform metrics.

Outcome

CRM and product analytics could speak to the same journey.

  • Braze ID and Account GUID specified for the GA data layer.
  • Braze ID carried in email URL parameters to preserve campaign context.
  • GTM audit complete for the relevant tag configuration.
  • Consent-aware configuration under TrustArc built into the measurement plan.
  • Recurring executive readouts supported on user behaviour and product engagement trends.

Design lesson

Lifecycle analytics needs an identity spine.

Without a cross-channel identity bridge, CRM analytics stops at open and click metrics while product analytics starts after arrival. The business question lives between those systems, so the measurement design has to connect them before the dashboard can.
