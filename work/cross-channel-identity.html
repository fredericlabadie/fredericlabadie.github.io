Case study · Lens 06 · Marketing Analytics · Cross-channel identity

Connecting email behaviour to in-app sessions.

The instrumentation backbone behind every "did this campaign actually drive product interaction or just a click?" question.

/ State-regulated retail platform · Braze + GA4 + web

→
What happened
A state-regulated retail platform's email campaigns and product analytics now share a unified identity layer — Braze ID and Account GUID pushed into the GA data layer, Braze ID carried in email URL parameters, GTM audit complete and tag configuration consent-aware under TrustArc. Every "did this campaign actually drive product interaction or just a click?" question can now be answered against a single user identity. The spec became the basis for recurring monthly executive readouts on user behaviour and product engagement trends.
Context
A state-regulated retail platform running Braze for email and GA4 for product behaviour with no identity bridge between them. Campaign clicks ended at the click; once the user landed in the app or on the web, the trail of who they were and what they did fragmented. Executive readouts wanted unified user-journey questions; the data layer couldn't answer them.
What I did
Authored the spec to push Braze ID and Account GUID into the GA data layer, and to carry Braze ID in email URL parameters — the cross-channel identity stitching that connects CRM behaviour (email opens, clicks) to app and web sessions for a unified user-journey view. Same engagement covered GTM audit, TrustArc consent-aware tag configuration, and the recurring monthly executive readout cycle the new identity layer powered.
