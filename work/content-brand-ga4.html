Case study · Lens 01 · Question Formulation

When the homepage isn't the front door.

A discovery process that surfaced six tracking gaps the client didn't know existed — and one finding that reframed the editorial strategy.

/ Content brand · freelance · 2022–2024

→
What happened
A high-profile content brand's GA installation went from traffic numbers without answers to a GA4 stack that surfaced six tracking gaps the client did not know existed, reframed the editorial premise (podcast pages — not the homepage — were where the most engaged audience actually lived), and shipped with a Loom training library so the team could operate it without the analyst. Adoption signal: the agency returned with substantive questions in October 2022, and came back unprompted two years later (April 2024) with a GTM issue. The relationship was still live — the only adoption metric that matters.
Most analytics installations are correct without being useful. The gap between traffic numbers and answers is rarely about data volume — it's about whether the schema you instrumented maps to the questions the business actually asks. Adoption, not the dashboard, is the deliverable.
Situation
A high-profile content brand (podcasts, newsletter, events, books) with GA installed but unable to answer the questions that drove its business — whether people were actually listening, what drove newsletter signups, which pages were genuinely performing. The implicit editorial model treated the homepage as the primary entry point. Optimisation effort and content decisions both flowed from that premise.
Task
Move the client from data without answers to instrumentation that mapped to the questions actually being asked — and design it so a small editorial team could operate the implementation without the analyst. Two layers: surface the technical gaps (the data not being collected honestly), then rebuild around real editorial goals (podcast engagement, newsletter conversion, content-level dimensions). Adoption was the deliverable, not the dashboard.
Action
Four-step protocol (detail in the flow below). Structured discovery — documented Q&A with agency and client on what the data was/wasn't doing and what decisions it should inform. Audit against the questions — six technical gaps surfaced (double-firing pageviews, broken UTMs, no conversion tracking, podcast engagement entirely unmeasured, event taxonomy misaligned, iframe player events firing incorrectly). Full GA4 rebuild around real goals — scroll depth, episode engagement, 2-min engaged-session conversion, newsletter signups, content-level custom dimensions, source/medium fixed. Loom training library — custom dimensions, conversions, explorations, entrances/exits, real-time — so the team could operate the implementation without the analyst.
Result
Once the instrumentation was honest, the editorial premise reframed: podcast episode pages (not the homepage) were where the most engaged users were spending time — entering directly, staying longer, taking more actions than homepage visitors. The homepage-first model didn't match the audience that actually existed. Adoption signal: the agency returned in October 2022 with substantive usage questions — not service-questions, usage-questions. Two years later (April 2024), the same agency came back unprompted with a GTM issue. The implementation was being used, not shelved. That's the only adoption metric that matters.
