// 01 / thesisglobal analytics brief / buried finding

Core argument

A global analytics brief. One finding that changed the platform roadmap.

The primary engagement was measurement — GA analytics for a global digital brochure programme across multiple vehicle models and distributor regions. The most significant output was not the scheduled reporting. It was a specific load-time finding: nearly two-thirds of desktop users abandoned the Guided Tour before it loaded.

What happened

A measurement engagement that surfaced a structural product failure.

A global automotive OEM ran a digital eBrochure programme as the primary online product-information experience across distributor regions worldwide. The engagement covered reach, engagement, and CTA performance across multiple vehicle models. The critical finding — that load time was producing a 67% exit rate before users entered the Guided Tour on desktop — was not part of the original brief. It surfaced during deeper engagement analysis and moved from the analytics report to the platform architecture discussion.

Context

Digital brochures as primary product experience, measured via GA across a global distributor network.

eBrochures were the primary consumer-facing digital product for each vehicle — interactive content designed to support the consideration phase across global markets. Traffic arrived from multiple sources: direct, organic search, referral from distributor sites, and paid. Reporting was structured around reach, engagement depth, CTA click-through, and model-by-model performance across distributors.

Task

Report accurately. Then surface what the client didn’t know to ask about.

GA analytics and reporting across the programme — model-year retrospectives, distributor-level analysis, and ongoing measurement. The engagement extended beyond scheduled reporting to finding what the programme didn’t know to ask about.

// 02 / MY18 findingsreach growth / engagement flat / load time

MY18 retrospective

Reach was growing. Engagement was flat. And something was blocking the tour.

MY18 reached significantly more users than MY17, with mobile as the primary growth driver. Overall engagement metrics were flat — the highest-volume model’s engagement declined, which dragged the average. The load-time finding was not in the original brief.

// 03 / findingload time / platform roadmap / personalization

Critical finding

A load-time metric that belonged on the product roadmap, not the analytics report.

Exit rate before a page loads is a measurement problem before it is a UX problem. Once the cause was confirmed as load time, the finding moved from the analytics report to the platform architecture discussion for MY19.

The finding

Two-thirds of desktop users never saw the tour.

The desktop Guided Tour required a significant initial load before users could begin interacting. A 67% exit rate before the tour loaded meant the majority of desktop users directed to the product experience never received it. For a programme designed to drive consideration and CTA conversion, a front-loaded experience failure was a structural problem, not a content problem.

The finding was brought into the MY19 platform discussion as the primary argument for a structural improvement to the desktop load architecture. A programme-level eBrochure redesign and platform modernisation was initiated for MY19 on the back of this analysis.

Personalization strategy

Different entry points have different purposes. The data suggested the tour design should reflect that.

Analysis of traffic sources showed users arriving via different channels had measurably different behaviour patterns. Organic search arrivals, direct traffic, and email campaign arrivals each showed distinct engagement patterns — different pages consumed, different time-on-content, different CTA preferences. The MY17–18 behavioural data supported a personalisation approach: tailoring the tour’s opening content and CTA structure to entry source, using the channel-behaviour split as the design basis for MY19.

The proposal used traffic-source analysis, engagement depth data, and an observation about Guided Tour visitor intent: these users tended to arrive with a specific vehicle or feature in mind, not to browse. That made entry-point personalisation more plausible than it would be for general content pages, and the data supported structuring the pitch to the MY19 platform team around it.
