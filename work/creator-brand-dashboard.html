The migration had to be live by Monday.

A two-phase analytics engagement for a creator business education brand: KPI discovery, GTM implementation, email attribution, and dashboard build in August 2021 — then called back fourteen months later for emergency migration support across four-plus domains.

// 01 / thesistrust is earned in phase one

Core argument

The callback was the proof: the implementation was worth preserving.

When a client calls you back fourteen months later with a 48-hour deadline, the first engagement did something right.

What happened

A trusted measurement layer had to survive a platform migration.

A creator business education brand's managing agency brought me in to build a unified measurement layer: KPI and traffic-source discovery, GTM tagging, UTM discipline for email campaigns, goal setup, a unified dashboard, and a training walkthrough for the agency team. The work shipped and the agency could operate it.

Fourteen months later, the brand migrated from Squarespace 7 to 7.1 across four-plus domains. Analytics had to be live the Monday after the Sunday-night domain flip, and I was brought in for emergency migration support under a tight turnaround. The agency's note: "we should have brought you in earlier."

Situation

A creator brand with email campaigns and no reliable attribution.

The brand ran templates, courses, and resources, with email as a primary acquisition channel. GA was installed but the team could not reliably answer where traffic came from, which campaigns converted, or how to read their multi-domain data. The data was distributed across multiple properties in ways that made comparison unreliable.

Task

Build a measurement layer the agency could read, and email attribution that held up.

The brief had two layers: technical (GTM tagging, UTM structure, goal setup, multi-property consolidation) and human (a dashboard and walkthrough that made the data accessible to an agency team without a dedicated analytics function).

// 02 / engagement arcdiscovery / build / training / migration

Method

Phase one built the layer. Phase two tested how much it mattered.

A platform migration under deadline is a trust exercise as much as a technical one. The agency called because the measurement layer was worth preserving.

1KPI and traffic-source discovery

Facilitated a session to document incoming traffic sources, understand the traffic flows, and identify the key performance indicators the brand and agency actually needed to answer.

2GTM tagging and UTM architecture

Implemented tagging requirements, established UTM discipline for email campaigns, and reviewed the outgoing campaign structure so attribution would hold.

3Dashboard build and agency training

Built a unified dashboard and walked the agency through it so they could read, navigate, and explain the data to the client without ongoing analyst support.

4Emergency migration, four domains, 48 hours

When the site migrated platforms, GA4 and Universal Analytics had to be live across four-plus domains by Monday morning. Analytics was live when the site went public.

Action

The second engagement was possible because the first one was clean.

The Phase 1 work was complete enough that the agency operated independently for fourteen months. When the platform migration introduced a tracking risk, the agency knew what they had to protect and who to call. The migration scope included verifying which secondary domains needed analytics carried over, confirming access to a QA version of the new site before go-live, and getting everything confirmed before the Sunday-night domain flip.

// 03 / resultlong-tail trust / emergency callback / sustained relationship

Result

The long tail of a well-built implementation is that it becomes worth protecting.

An agency operating your dashboard independently for fourteen months before calling with a deadline is a stronger signal than a client thank-you email.

Outcome

Dashboard delivered, migration completed, relationship sustained.

  • Unified GA dashboard built and handed off with a walkthrough so the agency could operate it independently.
  • UTM discipline established for email campaigns, making traffic-source attribution reliable for the first time.
  • 14-month independent operation by the agency before Phase 2 was needed.
  • Emergency migration support brought analytics live by Monday after a Sunday-night domain flip across four-plus domains.
  • Agency called back under deadline with the note: "we should have brought you in earlier" — evidence the implementation was considered business-critical.

Design lesson

The callback signals what the first engagement was worth.

When a client brings you back to preserve an analytics layer during a platform migration, it is because someone in the organisation understands what they would lose without it. That understanding was built in Phase 1 by making the data legible to the agency team, not just technically correct.
