Case study · Lens 06 · Marketing Analytics · Funnel analytics

A 50%+ candidate drop-off, decomposed step by step.

Turning a qualitative problem into a solvable product question.

/ Global AI services firm · candidate sourcing platform · 2025

→
What happened
A global AI services firm's sourcing team moved from "we're losing candidates somewhere" to "step 4 has a 43% abandonment rate; let's look at what's happening there" — via full GA4 instrumentation of the Candidate Management Platform and a Looker Studio dashboard on the GA4 → BigQuery pipeline that replaced the manual Excel workflow. Presented to sourcing stakeholders in a May 2025 sync framed as "an update on what data is now available for sourcing decisions." Qualitative problem decomposed into a solvable product question.
Context
A global AI services firm's sourcing team knew there was a 50%+ drop-off between candidate selection and start of work but couldn't tell where. Metrics lived in Excel spreadsheets hand-populated from the operational system — no funnel decomposition was possible, and any conversation about fixing the drop-off was stuck at the qualitative level.
What I did
Led front-end behavioural instrumentation of the Candidate Management Platform with two web engineers. GA4 event capture across application start, exam steps, compliance checkpoints, and selection status, with user-identity parameters enabling per-candidate and per-programme segmentation. Built a Looker Studio dashboard on the GA4 → BigQuery pipeline that replaced the manual Excel workflow with a queryable view of candidate flow. Presented to sourcing stakeholders as available data for sourcing decisions, not as a finding to react to.
