// 01 / thesisbilling metering is not product analytics

Core argument

Backend metering could bill usage. It could not explain behaviour.

The platform needed a front-end signal layer to answer which copilots were used, by whom, how often, and what product behaviour looked like.

What happened

An enterprise AI platform gained a behavioural analytics pipeline.

An enterprise AI copilot platform built on Anthropic's Claude had scaled from internal tool to commercial product but had only backend metering for billing. I designed and delivered the front-end behavioural signal layer: GA4 event collection and BigQuery export covering platform-wide and organisation-specific events.

The pipeline was confirmed live and in use by engineering teams within weeks. I continued afterward as an internal BigQuery SME, including a GA4-querying primer for engineers beginning to work with the data.

Context

The product had usage metering, not product behaviour.

Backend billing metering could support commercial operations, but it could not answer product questions about which copilots were being used, how often, by whom, or how behaviour differed across organisations.

Task

Add a front-end behavioural layer without making engineering dependent on analytics.

The work needed to produce usable event data, export it to BigQuery, and equip the engineering team to query it directly rather than waiting on an analyst for every answer.

// 02 / pipelinefront end / GA4 / BigQuery / engineering enablement

Build layer

The pipeline was only half the deliverable.

The other half was making the data legible to the people building the platform.

1Event layer

Designed GA4 event collection for platform-wide and organisation-specific behavioural events.

2BigQuery export

Delivered the export pipeline that made front-end events available for deeper analysis.

3KPI queries

Prepared examples including total copilot count, conversations per user, and Custom Copilot access-type distribution.

4Advisory layer

Provided advisory input to the platform's adversarial-testing tool on its own measurement layer.

Action

The data layer was designed for engineers to use.

I delivered the GA4 event collection and BigQuery export pipeline, then supported engineering adoption with a querying primer covering event-parameter unnesting, the schema model, and KPI queries. The goal was not only to capture behaviour but to make it available to the team building the platform.

// 03 / resultlive pipeline / engineering adoption / platform measurement

Result

The product gained the behavioural layer billing data could not provide.

Commercial usage metering and product analytics answer different questions. This work added the latter.

Outcome

Engineering could query how the platform was actually being used.

  • GA4 event layer delivered for platform-wide and organisation-specific behavioural events.
  • BigQuery export pipeline confirmed live and in use by engineering within weeks.
  • GA4 querying primer created for the engineering team.
  • Internal BigQuery SME support continued after delivery.
  • Measurement advisory extended to the platform's adversarial-testing tool.

Design lesson

AI product analytics still starts with ordinary behavioural questions.

Even on a Claude-powered product, the core questions are familiar: who used what, how often, in which context, and what changed after release. The platform may be novel; the need for trustworthy behavioural telemetry is not.
