# Frederic Labadie Portfolio — AI Design Brief

## Site type

Personal portfolio for an Amsterdam-based Analytics Architect, data strategy professional, and digital measurement leader.

## Primary audience

Recruiters, hiring managers, analytics leaders, product leaders, marketing technology teams, UX-adjacent teams, and AI/design tools reviewing the portfolio structure.

## Desired impression

Senior, analytical, precise, evidence-led, editorial, and technically credible. The site should feel like an analytical dossier, technical field notebook, or evidence board rather than a generic startup portfolio.

## Current positioning

Frederic Labadie is a full-time Analytics Architect at Telus Digital. His best-fit areas are analytics architecture, product telemetry, analytics engineering, UX telemetry, and marketing analytics. He is Amsterdam-based and has EU work authorisation.

## Visual language

Blueprint, technical dossier, analytical field notes, case-library archive, and evidence board.

## Content architecture

The portfolio is intentionally organized by problem shape rather than employer chronology. The case library includes seven lenses:

- AI workflows
- Data engineering
- Instrumentation
- Adoption and enablement
- Marketing analytics
- Experimentation
- Question discovery

## Current proof points to preserve

- 83% alert-noise reduction in V1 production AI alert triage.
- 38 hours of analyst time recovered per month.
- Approximately 50% BigQuery pipeline cost reduction.
- +10.8% year-over-year CTR lift from CDP migration.
- 14 years analytics experience.
- 11 Amplitude credentials.
- MSc Data Analytics.
- EU work authorisation.

## Selected case themes to preserve

- Shadow IT / compatibility configurator: the data surfaced the gap, but frontline staff explained why.
- State-regulated retail BI enablement: the reporting layer survived the team that built it.
- Mixed-methods Stanley Steemer research: telemetry located the problem; method diagnosed the user gap.

## Do not change

- Preserve the evidence-led tone.
- Preserve the case-library structure.
- Preserve the homepage’s “measurement problem” positioning.
- Preserve the distinction between proof points, case studies, and methodology.
- Preserve the muted, professional, blueprint-like palette.
- Avoid generic SaaS-template gloss.
- Avoid over-polished personal-brand language that weakens the analytical voice.
- Avoid making the work sound like vague strategy; keep the measurement, systems, and adoption language.

## Design task guidance

When proposing improvements, prioritize:

1. readability
2. recruiter scan speed
3. case-study hierarchy
4. proof-point clarity
5. mobile usability
6. accessibility
7. preserving the existing voice
8. making the case library easier for AI/design tools to interpret

## Interpretation notes for AI/design tools

Read the portfolio as evidence of judgment, systems thinking, instrumentation skill, stakeholder adoption, analytics architecture, and mixed-methods reasoning. The strongest design improvements should make that evidence easier to scan without flattening the site into a generic resume page.
